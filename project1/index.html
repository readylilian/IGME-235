BeeGame

A barebones set up of a bee-based dating game. This project was created independantly as part of Wichack, a 24-hour hackathon. BeeGame is an educational dating game with the goal of encouraging people to learn about the various native (and invasive) bees of North America. Adding a base-building aspect to the standard dating game format, you'll have to choose not only the correct option to charm the various bees, but tend to your garden and provide the habitat the various bees prefer.

Tater Tots

A side-scrolling, level-based running game. You play as a mutated potato running from all those who wish to eat your delicious potato goodness. Running through the farm you were born, the spaceship that kidnapped you and the alien planets that you escape to. Written in C# with Monogame in a team of four for a class project. I spearheaded organization of the project and sprints, along with all file interaction, player abilities and movement, along with design and implementation of the potato and powerups.

About Me

I'm a Game Developer and Computer Programmer from just about everywhere in Colorado. I started learning to code in sixth grade (when I was allowed to buy my first video game) and haven't left either alone since. I was lucky enough to be able to teach and tutor kids in Unity over the summer and be the same positive influence in programming that my teachers were to me at that age.

As a current student of RIT I'm pursuing my bachelor's degree in both Game Design and Development and Computer Science. I've really enjoyed the opportunity to expand my skills out into all the fields that go into making a game, from project management and programming to animation.

Outside of class I'm an active member of Computer Science House, and love to go to the various seminars and events happening every night. CSH has been a huge part of my experience at RIT and they've really encouraged me to branch out past class assignments and to pursue my own interests and projects. I'll always be grateful that I'm able to immediately reach out to a community that can tell me exactly why I'll need a stronger motor than a hex bug's to get a furby to walk.

Devcade

This one's a CSH project and my first time using React. Devcade as a project seeks to create an arcade cabinet with cloud capability. People will be able to upload their games to the Devcade server through our website (that's what I'm in charge of making). Then, on the cabinet download those games to play, and save your personal file to your account with the website. As of right now there are four teams in charge of the separate aspects of the cabinet, and as a part of the website team I'll be the front end person, with my two teammates working server-side.

Starfall

The Witch lived a very peacefull life in a tree a few miles from town. Tending to her garden, practicing a variety of household spells, and laying out in the starlight to recharge her power. That was until, the stars fell out of the sky. The Witch now has to descend into the caverns below to collect the stars and return them to the sky, or risk losing her powers forever. This one was a class project and is now the reason why I don't have any Git commits over the summer. I really love the idea I've got for this game's combat system and put any spare time I had this summer into getting the basics of this working so I can have a demo!
